About Heart Risk and Why You Should Test

Heart disease is a leading cause of death globally, and understanding your risk can help prevent heart attacks and other cardiovascular issues.

Factors like age, smoking, blood pressure, and cholesterol levels play a major role in assessing your heart risk. Early detection can lead to lifestyle changes or medical interventions that reduce risk.

Meaning of the Form Terms

  • Age: The risk of heart disease increases with age, particularly after 50 years.
  • Gender: Males generally have a higher risk, but post-menopausal women also face increased risk.
  • Systolic/Diastolic Blood Pressure: Higher blood pressure indicates more force against artery walls, increasing heart disease risk.
  • Cholesterol: High cholesterol levels can lead to plaque buildup in arteries, increasing heart attack risk.
  • Smoking: Smoking greatly increases the risk of heart disease and stroke.
  • Diabetes: Diabetes is a major risk factor for cardiovascular diseases.
